This area keeps you on top of your work in LP-DevTool: **My Scripts** lists every project you have
saved, and the **Activity** view shows a chronological record of what you have done recently.

## My Scripts

The **My Scripts** page is your central project overview. Every script you create and save in the
builder appears here: as its own card with the key details at a glance.

Above the list you can see how many **projects** you have in total. Scripts are sorted by their
last edit date, so your most recently worked-on project sits at the top.

### What a script card shows

Each card summarizes one saved script:

- **Name** of the script
- **Framework badge**: marks the target platform, e.g. **Standalone**, **ESX** or **QBCore**
- **Updated**: the date you last changed the script
- **Open**: a button that loads the script directly in the builder

### Reopen a saved script

<Steps>
<Step title="Find the project">
Locate the card of the script you want in the overview. Since recently edited projects appear at
the top, your latest script is usually visible right away.
</Step>

<Step title="Click Open">
Click **Open** on the card. The script loads in the builder with all of its saved settings, so you
pick up exactly where you left off.
</Step>

<Step title="Keep working and save">
Make your changes in the builder. When you save, the **Updated** date refreshes and the project
moves up in the overview.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Create a new script

In the top right you'll find the **New Script** button. It opens the builder with an empty project.
As soon as you save it, it automatically appears in your overview.

<Note>
Until you have saved your first script, the page shows an empty state with the message
**"No scripts yet"** and a button to create your first script right away.
</Note>

## Activity

The **Activity** page gives you an overview of your actions: searchable and filterable by time
range. This lets you trace what you did and when, at any time.

It shows your most recent entries in chronological order (newest first).

### Key figures at a glance

Above the history you see three metrics about your activity:

- **Total**: the number of all displayed entries
- **Today**: actions from the last 24 hours
- **Last 7 days**: actions from the past week

### Search and filter

<Steps>
<Step title="Search by text">
Type into the **"Search activity …"** field to narrow the history down to entries that contain your
term. The list updates as you type.
</Step>

<Step title="Pick a time range">
Use the filter buttons to choose a range: **All**, **24 hours**, **7 days** or **30 days**. Only
actions within the selected window are then shown.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The search and the time-range filter work together. If the selection returns no matches, the
message **"No activity for this selection."** appears: clear the search term or widen the range.
</Note>

### How the history is structured

Entries are grouped by day, with headings such as **Today**, **Yesterday** or the respective date.
Each entry consists of:

- an **icon** that automatically matches the type of action (e.g. saving, export/download or
  script-related actions),
- the **description text** of the action,
- a **relative timestamp** such as "just now", "5 min ago" or "2 days ago".

<Note>
Hover over the timestamp to reveal the exact moment (date and time) as a tooltip.
</Note>

<Card title="Tip" icon="lightbulb">
Use Activity as a quick memory aid: search for a project name, for example, to see when you last
exported or saved it.
</Card>
