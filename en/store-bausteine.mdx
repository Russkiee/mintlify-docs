The **Store** is the place in LP-DevTool where you discover ready-made templates and building blocks. Instead of starting every script from scratch, you check here for what already exists: from the team and the community: and use it as orientation and a starting point for your own script in the [Script Builder](/en/script-builder).

You open the store via the **Store** entry in the sidebar. The store is only visible to signed-in users.

## Templates and plugins

The store is split into two areas:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Templates" icon="boxes">
    Ready-made script setups you use as a basis for your own script. Each template targets a framework.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Plugins" icon="puzzle">
    Individual building blocks you add to your script. Each plugin has a category and a usage area (Client, Server or NUI).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Both areas are shown below one another. Next to each heading you see a small number: it shows how many entries currently match your search and filter.

## Browsing: step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the store">
    Click **Store** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search">
    Type into the search box at the top: **"Search templates & plugins …"**. The lists are filtered instantly as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by framework">
    Use the filter chips to narrow the templates down: **All**, **Standalone**, **ESX**, **QBCore** or **Qbox**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review an entry">
    Look at the name, description and badges on each card to decide what fits your plan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Search

The search box narrows both areas at once. It is case-insensitive.

- **Templates** are searched by **name** and **description**.
- **Plugins** are searched by **label** and **category**.

If nothing matches, a note appears such as *"No matching templates found."* or *"No matching plugins found."*. In that case clear the search box or adjust your search term.

## Filter by framework

The filter chips above the lists restrict the templates to a framework:

| Chip | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **All** | Shows templates of every framework. |
| **Standalone** | Plain FiveM with no framework dependency. |
| **ESX** | Templates for the ESX framework. |
| **QBCore** | Templates for QBCore. |
| **Qbox** | Templates for Qbox. |

<Note>
  The framework filter only affects the **templates**. You narrow the **plugins** down using the search alone.
</Note>

## Understanding templates

Each template is shown as a card with the following details:

- **Name**: the template's label.
- **Framework badge**: shows which framework the template is intended for (**Standalone**, **ESX**, **QBCore** or **Qbox**). If no framework is set, **Standalone** applies.
- **Description**: a short explanation of what the template does. If a template has no description, it shows *"No description available."*.

This lets you quickly find a template that matches your framework and your goal.

## Understanding plugins

Plugins are the individual building blocks. Each plugin card shows:

- **Icon**: a small symbol for quick recognition.
- **Label**: the plugin's name.
- **Category**: the plugin's classification. If no category is set, it shows **Community**.
- **Usage badge**: where the plugin runs:

| Badge | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Client** | Runs client-side on the player. |
| **Server** | Runs server-side. |
| **NUI** | Relates to the NUI / interface layer. |

## Using it as a basis for your own script

The store is your starting point: get an overview of which templates and building blocks already exist, and build your own script on top of them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a match">
    Search and filter the store for a template or plugin that fits your plan and your framework.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue in the Script Builder">
    Assemble your script from blocks in the [Script Builder](/en/script-builder): using what you saw in the store as a template and orientation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and build">
    Save your script to your account and generate the finished FiveM resource via **Build & Download**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Good to know

- The store only shows **public** templates and plugins from the team and community.
- Entries are sorted by **creation date**: newest first.
- Search and the framework filter can be combined; the counters next to the headings update live.
