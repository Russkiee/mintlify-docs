The **Notification Builder** lets you design your own notification style for your FiveM server. You define the look, position, colors, icons, animation and display duration, watch the result in a live preview, and export a ready-to-use resource as a ZIP file. The generated resource hooks into ESX (`es_extended`) and automatically replaces its standard notifications with your design.

<Note>
The **Description** field in the builder is for preview only. The actual text of a notification comes at runtime from the script that triggers it (e.g. "Received $15,000"). In the builder you design the appearance, not the specific message texts.
</Note>

## The three variants

A notification has three variants, each of which you can style completely independently. Switch between them using the tabs above the preview:

- **Information**: neutral hints
- **Success**: successfully completed actions
- **Error**: failed actions or warnings

<Note>
If a script triggers a notification of type `warning`, `danger` or `inform`, it is mapped automatically: `inform` uses the **Information** variant, while `warning` and `danger` use the **Error** variant. Unknown types fall back to **Information**. So by styling three variants you cover all incoming types.
</Note>

## Step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set a project name">
    Enter a name in the top left. It is later used as the filename of the exported ZIP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally load a template">
    Pick one of the included designs from the **Template** dropdown as a starting point. All settings are applied and remain fully editable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a position">
    Use **Position** to decide where notifications appear on screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select and style a variant">
    Switch between **Information**, **Success** and **Error** via the tabs and adjust content, colors, animation and more for each variant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the preview">
    Click **Play test** to check the entrance animation of the active variant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export">
    Click **Build & Download** to download the finished resource as a ZIP.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Position

The position applies globally to all variants. Six anchor points are available:

- Top left, Top center, Top right
- Bottom left, Bottom center, Bottom right

<Note>
For the bottom positions, notifications that appear at the same time stack from bottom to top, so the newest one always sits at the screen edge.
</Note>

## Settings in detail

The **Width** (250-480 px) and the **Position** apply to the entire notification. All other settings can be configured separately per variant.

### Content

- **Title**: the heading (1-48 characters). It is shown in uppercase in the final notification.
- **Description (preview)**: for the builder preview only (up to 160 characters). The real text comes from the script at runtime.
- **Icon**: pick from nine symbols: Info, Check, Cross, Warning, Shield, Bell, Ban, Bolt and Star.

### Layout

- **Width**: 250-480 px (global)
- **Height**: 50-150 px
- **Display duration**: 1000-12000 ms (in 500 ms steps); also controls the progress bar that runs out along the bottom edge.

### Background

- **Type**: **Solid** or **Gradient**.
- Solid: a single **color**.
- Gradient: **gradient start**, **gradient end** and **gradient angle** (0-360°).
- **Opacity**: 0-100 %.

### Border & shadow

- **Corner radius**: 0-32 px
- **Border width**: 0-6 px
- **Border color**
- **Shadow glow**: 0-40 px
- **Shadow color**

### Accent bar

- **Show accent**: toggle the colored bar on the side of the notification.
- **Accent color**
- **Accent width**: 2-12 px

### Icon style

- **Icon background**: background color of the icon tile.
- **Icon symbol color**: color of the symbol itself.

### Typography

- **Title size**: 10-24 px
- **Title color**
- **Text size**: 9-18 px
- **Text color**

### Animation

- **Entrance**: entrance animation: Slide (right), Slide (left), Slide (top), Slide (bottom), Fade or Zoom.
- **Duration**: 100-2000 ms.
- **Easing**: Ease (default), Linear, Ease-In, Ease-Out, Ease-In-Out or Custom.

<Note>
With the **Custom** easing, four additional bezier sliders (X1, Y1, X2, Y2) appear for a fully custom easing curve. X1/X2 range from 0-1, Y1/Y2 from -0.5-1.5.
</Note>

### Transform

Fine spatial adjustments of the notification container:

- **Scale**: 0.5-1.5
- **Offset X** / **Offset Y**: -100-100 px each
- **Rotation**: -180-180°
- **Skew X** / **Skew Y**: -45-45° each

## Preview & test

The live preview always shows the currently selected variant. Switch the variant via the tabs, and use **Play test** to replay the entrance animation together with the running progress bar. This lets you check animation, duration and position before exporting.

## Build & Download

**Build & Download** generates the finished FiveM resource and downloads it as a ZIP (named after your project). The archive contains everything needed: `fxmanifest.lua`, the client script and the NUI files (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) with your design.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Unzip the file">
    Extract the downloaded file into your server's `resources` folder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the resource">
    Add the resource to your `server.cfg` (e.g. `ensure your_resource_name`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restart the server">
    Restart the server or load the resource. Your design is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using it in-game

The generated resource hooks into ESX (`es_extended`) automatically: existing ESX notifications (`ESX.ShowNotification`, the `ESX:Notify` event, and `esx:showNotification` / `esx:showAdvancedNotification`) are rendered in your design without any further changes. You do not need to rewrite your existing scripts.

For a quick in-game check, the command `/lpnotify_test` is available, which triggers a test notification of the Information type.

<Note>
The resource requires **es_extended** on the server.
</Note>

## Reset

Use **Reset** at any time to restore the builder's default configuration and start over.
